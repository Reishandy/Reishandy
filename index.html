Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 27, 2026

The General Philosophy

I build software with a strict respect for your personal space. My approach to privacy is simple and uncompromising: I do not harvest, sell, or monetize your data.

Whenever possible, data is processed and stored entirely locally on your device. In cases where my applications interact with a server, it is strictly to provide core functionality. I do not implement third-party analytics tracking, I do not log telemetry, and I do not build profiles on my users.

If an app doesn't need your data to function, it doesn't ask for it.

Your iBeacon Simulator

Your iBeacon Simulator requires specific permissions to function correctly, and it is important you understand why they are needed and how they are used.

Location & Background Access

This app requests "Always" location access. This is a technical requirement imposed by iOS to utilize Core Location's region monitoring for iBeacons in the background. Without this permission, the app cannot automatically check you in when you approach a room while the app is closed or your phone is locked.

Local Processing

Your precise location data (GPS coordinates) is never tracked, stored, or transmitted to any server. The app only listens for the presence of specific, predefined Bluetooth iBeacons. When an iBeacon is detected, the app sends a stateless, anonymous payload to the local server to confirm your presence in that specific room. No continuous location history is maintained.
