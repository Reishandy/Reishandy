Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 27, 2026

The General Philosophy

I build software with a strict respect for your personal space. My approach to privacy is simple and uncompromising: I do not harvest, sell, or monetize your data.

Whenever possible, data is processed and stored entirely locally on your device. I do not implement third-party analytics tracking, I do not log usage telemetry, and I do not build profiles on my users.

Data Sharing

I do not share any personal information with third parties. Because I do not collect data, there is no information to disclose, sell, or transfer.

Security

Because my applications prioritize local processing, the risk of data exposure is minimized. I employ industry-standard practices to ensure that any local data you store remains strictly within your control.

Children's Privacy

My applications are not directed toward children under the age of 13. I do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under the age of 13.

Your iBeacon Simulator

Your iBeacon Simulator is a developer utility designed to assist with testing and prototyping proximity-based features. It requires specific permissions to operate, and I am committed to complete transparency regarding these requirements.

Bluetooth & Location Access

This app requests Bluetooth and Location access solely to enable the broadcast (transmission) and discovery (ranging) of iBeacons. These permissions are strictly required by the iOS operating system to interface with the hardware necessary to simulate iBeacon signals for your testing environment.

Local Processing

Your precise location data (GPS coordinates) is never tracked, stored, or transmitted to any server. The app only listens for the presence of specific, predefined Bluetooth iBeacons. No continuous location history is maintained. All simulation data is transient and exists only while the application is active.

Updates to this Policy

I may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in my development practices. I will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page.
